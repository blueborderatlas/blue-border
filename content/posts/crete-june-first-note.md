Crete immediately felt too large for a quick opinion. Some islands let you pretend you have understood them after a few bus rides and one good beach. Crete resists that. It stretches out, dry and bright, with distances that make the map feel slightly dishonest.

June brought heat, but also a looseness that suited the island. Roads moved between towns, olive trees, empty-looking hills and sudden coastal openings. The sea could be clear and inviting, then the next hour belonged to dust, shade and the search for water.

I liked the feeling of not being able to finish it. Crete seems better as a slow project: one base, then another, then a beach found because the day went differently than planned.

The first note is simple. Do not arrive with a small-island mindset. Leave space for buses, long walks, ordinary tavernas, and the quiet fatigue that comes from being near the sea all day.
