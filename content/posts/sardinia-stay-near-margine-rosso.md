# A Quiet Stay Near the Sea in Southern Sardinia

During our time in southern Sardinia, we stayed in a small independent guest room near Margine Rosso beach.

Compared with a normal hotel, the place felt much more personal and relaxed. White walls, palm trees, a quiet pool area, and slow evenings near the sea made the entire trip feel calmer.

The room itself was simple but comfortable, with large windows, blackout curtains, fast Wi-Fi, a mini fridge, and a private bathroom. The entrance connected directly to the garden, so coming and going always felt very free and private.

What we remember most, though, was the atmosphere created by the hosts.

On the first night we arrived quite late, and almost every nearby restaurant was already closed. The host first recommended a local food delivery app, but later realized we could not register because we did not have an Italian phone number.

At that point it was already close to 10 PM.

In the end, he offered to order food for us himself so we could still have dinner after arriving.

During the following days, he often translated his Italian messages into Chinese to make communication easier for us. Sometimes the translations were imperfect, but it actually made the experience feel even more genuine and warm.

He also patiently explained how to use the jacuzzi step by step, including how to activate it, how long it would run, and how to close everything correctly afterward.

Originally the jacuzzi required an extra fee, but later they decided not to charge us for it.

None of these things were luxurious in an obvious way, but together they made the stay feel genuinely welcoming.

The location was also surprisingly convenient even without renting a car.

There was a local bus stop within walking distance, with direct connections to central Cagliari. Some parts of the route followed the coastline, and even simple bus rides started to feel like part of the trip itself.

For slower travel, Sardinia actually felt much easier than we expected.

From here it was also possible to continue toward places like Villasimius and Costa Rei along the southeastern coast.

Most days ended the same way: spending hours near the sea, then slowly returning to the house in the evening.

Compared with many highly designed vacation stays, this place felt quieter and more human.
