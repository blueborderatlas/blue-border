Tenerife in February did not feel like an escape from winter so much as a quiet argument with it. Germany was still grey in the body, the kind of cold that stays in sleeves and train platforms. Then the island appeared with dark rock, hard blue sky and sea that looked deeper than the season should allow.

The coast felt less soft than I expected. There were beaches, of course, but also black stones, wind, concrete swimming ladders and roads folding around dry hills. I liked that roughness. It kept the island from becoming only a warm-weather idea.

Most of the day was simple: walk until the light changed, stop for coffee, watch people sit close to the water without making a performance of it. The sea was not always friendly, but it was always present.

Tenerife feels useful as a winter note because it is warm without being sleepy. Buses move, towns keep their ordinary rhythm, and the coast has enough texture to make a short trip feel larger than the flight.
