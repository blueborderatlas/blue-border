Nice is not hidden, and it does not pretend to be. The sea is too bright, the promenade too sure of itself, the old town too ready to fill with voices. Still, there are quieter ways to meet it.

I think of Nice as a border feeling more than a checklist: France leaning toward Italy, train lines running along the coast, warm stone, market streets, coffee taken before the day becomes too polished. The water looks almost staged from above, but close up it is pebbles, ankles, towels and people adjusting to the light.

The best version of the day was simple. Walk early. Sit somewhere ordinary. Let the city become less glossy. Watch the coast as a route rather than a backdrop.

Nice belongs in the Italy archive here because it sits naturally beside Genoa, Liguria and the idea of moving along the same blue edge by train. Not exact geography, more travel rhythm.
