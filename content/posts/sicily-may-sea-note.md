Sicily in May feels like a threshold. The island is already bright, already warm in the middle of the day, already full of sea color, but it has not completely crossed into the heavy machinery of summer.

That in-between feeling is useful. You can still walk without surrendering the whole afternoon. Harbors feel active but not overwhelmed. Beaches begin to make sense, though the water may still ask for a small act of courage at first.

I keep returning to Sicily as a route as much as a destination. Palermo, Catania, Trapani, ferries, buses, old streets, cheap flights from Germany: the island keeps offering different ways to enter it.

This note is only a marker for now. Late May deserves more detail later: which coast felt easiest, where the wind mattered, where swimming felt natural, and where a simple evening near the water became the real point of the trip.
