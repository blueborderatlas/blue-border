Genoa does not present itself gently. It is vertical, compressed, a little dark in the lanes, and then suddenly open to cranes, ferries and water. I liked that it did not feel arranged only for visitors.

The city has a working coastline, not a decorative one. You notice trucks, ropes, ships, railings, apartment windows, coffee counters and people moving with the confidence of those who are not trying to be part of a postcard.

Walking here feels different from walking in a smaller coastal town. The sea is near, but the city keeps interrupting it. You earn glimpses instead of receiving a clean promenade.

That roughness gives Genoa its charm. A morning coffee can feel private even in a busy street. A short climb can change the whole shape of the city. The port is not always beautiful in the easy sense, but it gives the place weight.

This is a note to return to Genoa slowly, with fewer plans and better shoes.
