Zakynthos has water that almost makes writing difficult. It is too blue in the obvious way, too ready to flatten the place into a single image. The first task is to let the island become more than that.

I noticed the movement around the coast: boats leaving, cars stopping at viewpoints, sandals on hot ground, people negotiating shade. The sea was the center, but not the whole story.

A good beach day here probably needs less ambition than the internet suggests. One place, enough water, enough patience to watch the light move. The island feels better when it is not treated like a sequence of photo stops.

This is only a draft note for now. I want to return to the quieter parts: early walks, small harbors, the road back from the beach when everyone is sun-tired and the sky begins to soften.
