Santorini is almost too visible before you arrive. The image arrives first: white walls, blue domes, sunset crowds, expensive terraces. It takes a little time to let the real island push through the postcard.

The quieter moments were not dramatic. A coffee before the heat sharpened. A side street where the view disappeared. Wind moving dust across a path. The feeling of volcanic land under all the polished surfaces.

I do not think Santorini needs to be defended or dismissed. It is both beautiful and overheld by expectation. The trick is to stop asking it to be secret. Instead, look for pauses inside the famous shape.

Sunset is still sunset, even when everyone is waiting for it. But I liked the minutes afterward more: people leaving, light cooling, the island becoming less watched. That is where this draft begins.
