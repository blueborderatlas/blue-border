Fuerteventura feels wider than expected. The beaches do not ask much from you except time and a tolerance for wind. In winter, that can be enough.

The island has a stripped-down mood: pale sand, low buildings, dry land, Atlantic movement. It is not cozy in the usual sense. It clears space around you. A long walk can feel like the main activity, not because there is nothing else to do, but because the coast makes walking feel complete.

The wind changes the day. It can make sitting difficult, swimming brief, hair impossible and thoughts cleaner. I liked that. A beach without wind can become decoration. A windy beach stays alive.

This is a draft for future notes on where to stay without feeling isolated, how to move without overspending, and which stretches of coast feel best for slow winter walking.
